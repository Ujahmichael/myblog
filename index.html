A MUST READ STORY

I don't work for money, said Rich Dad. Money works for me.
But how is that possible? I asked, genuinely puzzled.

Because I make money work for me, Rich Dad explained patiently. I invest in assets that generate income, whether I'm actively working or not. That way, my money grows and multiplies without me having to trade my time for it.

I sat there, digesting his words. It was a revelation, a shift in perspective that challenged everything I had been taught about money and work. Rich Dad's philosophy was simple yet profound: instead of working hard for money, I could make money work hard for me.
From that day forward, I vowed to learn all I could about investing, entrepreneurship, and financial literacy. I realized that true wealth wasn't just about how much money you earned, but how effectively you managed and grew your assets.
Rich Dad had opened my eyes to a whole new way of thinking about money, and I was determined to follow in his footsteps.

about author

Robert Toru Kiyosaki (born April 8, 1947) is a Japanese-American businessman and author, known for the Rich Dad Poor Dad series of personal finance books. He is the founder of the Rich Dad Company, a private financial education company that provides personal finance and business education to people through books and videos, and Rich Global LLC, which filed for bankruptcy in 2012.[1] Since 2010, Kiyosaki was the subject of a class action suit filed by people who attended his seminars,[2][3] and the subject of investigative documentaries by the CBC,[4] WTAE-TV[5] and CBS News.[6] In January 2024, Kiyosaki revealed that he was more than $1 billion dollars in debt.[7][8]
